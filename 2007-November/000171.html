Jan Hugo Prins wrote:
> - A while back we had to do a big restore of a directory that hadn't 
> changed for a few months, but 2 days before that restore a lot of data 
> got lost and we keep a history of about 16 days for that area. When the 
> user tried to do the restore from rdiffweb he only got about half of his 
> data. I then did the same restore from the commandline with rdiff-backup 
> and I got all the data. This offcourse proofed to us that rdiff-backup 
> is doing what it should do, but it also shows us that we can't trust 
> rdiff-web to give full restores.
>   
Actually, rdiffWeb uses rdiff-backup to do restores - it asks 
rdiff-backup to restore the directory to a temporary folder, zips the 
folder up, and gives it back to the client. Can you reproduce the error? 
If you can get a test case, I'll be happy to fix it.

I would guess that what happened is that either there was not enough 
space in the temp folder, or rdiff-backup on the server encountered some 
other kind of error, and died half-way through the restore. rdiffWeb 
then blindly took the half-restored directory, thinking that it was 
completely restored.

I'm not sure why rdiffWeb doesn't do more thorough checking of whether 
rdiff-backup succeeded. That would be something worth checking into.

Thanks for the bug report!
