This is a nasty issue. CherryPy does a particularly bad job at 
maintaining API backwards compatibility, so supporting both 3.0 and 2.1 
would be very difficult. Many distros still package 2.1, and even when 
they move to 2.2 or 3.0, I'm hesitant to force users to use the latest 
version of their preferred distro to get native packages.

On the other hand, native https does seem rather appealing.

I'm thinking of holding off for another 6 or 8 months or so, then 
switching. Any other users have comments?
